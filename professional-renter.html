Professional Renter Guide

How to identify and avoid problematic tenants

What is a Professional Renter?

Most renters mean well; they move in, pay rent on time, and have an overall positive relationship with their landlords. Professional renters, however, are a different story.

These are people who treat renting like a game, seeing how much they can get away with (and how little they can pay) before being evicted and moving on to their next target.

1. Tenants Who Never Planned on Paying

  1. Every landlord's nightmare: the tenant who never had any intention of paying rent. Oftentimes this tenant looks good on paper, pays rent for a couple of months, and then shows their true colors.
  2. This might start as late payments that incrementally come to a stop altogether, or show up suddenly as a complete stoppage in rent altogether.

How to Avoid Tenants Who Don't Plan on Paying

Check tenants' credit scores, validate their employment and previous landlord references, check their vibe in person, and pay special attention to signs of unreliability. Some examples are: always letting you know that rent is going to be late, having consistent excuses for not being able to pay, or missing a payment without any explanation at all.

2. Tenants Who Rent Out Your Property Without Your Permission

  1. Another common scam is tenants posing as you, the landlord. After moving in, they might pretend to be the landlord of the unit, rent their space to other people, and pocket the rent themselves.
  2. If you're renting out a unit in a building with a concierge, these tenants also sometimes work out deals with the front desk people to allow illegal tenants in and out of the building without any trouble.

How to Avoid Tenants Who Rent Out Your Property Without Your Permission

Ask about any consistently short stays in rental histories, install a security system, and ask your concierge (if you have one) to let you know about any unauthorized guests.
